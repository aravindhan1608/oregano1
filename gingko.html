USES OF GINGKO

1.MEDICINAL PURPOSES

Ginkgo biloba has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries to treat various ailments. Modern research has also shown potential health benefits, including improving cognitive function, enhancing memory, reducing anxiety, and alleviating symptoms of conditions like dementia and Alzheimer's disease. Ginkgo supplements, typically made from the tree's leaves, are widely available in health stores and pharmacies.

2.LANDSCAPING AND ORNAMENTAL PLANTING

Ginkgo biloba is commonly planted as an ornamental tree in parks, gardens, and urban landscapes due to its distinctive fan-shaped leaves, golden autumn foliage, and overall hardiness. It is tolerant of various soil conditions and pollution, making it suitable for urban environments.

3.Food and Culinary Use

Ginkgo nuts, the seeds of the ginkgo tree, are edible and commonly used in Asian cuisine. The nuts are often roasted and eaten as a snack or used as an ingredient in dishes such as congee, soups, and desserts. However, it's essential to properly prepare the nuts as they contain substances that can be toxic if consumed raw.

4.TRADITIONAL MEDICINE

In addition to its use in Chinese medicine, ginkgo has also been used in other traditional medicine systems around the world. Extracts from ginkgo leaves, seeds, and nuts have been used to treat conditions such as asthma, bronchitis, tinnitus, and circulatory disorders.

5.PHYTOCHEMICAL RESEARCH

Ginkgo biloba contains various bioactive compounds, including flavonoids, terpenoids, and ginkgolides, which have attracted the interest of researchers for their potential pharmaceutical and nutraceutical applications. These compounds have antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and neuroprotective properties, making them subjects of study for developing new drugs and supplements.
